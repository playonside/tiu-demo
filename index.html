Trinity International University
Women's Soccer

Play for a higher purpose.

We play for a higher purpose than winning. We play to glorify God, to advance the sport, and to create the most meaningful competitive athletic experience of our lives.

In pursuit of our purpose we adhere to guiding principles and it is principles, not outcomes, that define the culture of our team.

Winning is a byproduct of a commitment to living and playing according to our principles.

Purpose

Advance the sport of soccer; in particular, at the small college level.

Raise the bar with regard to style of play:

  • Possession & Precise Passing
  • High Rhythm & Tempo of Play - Want the Ball & Keep the Ball
  • Dynamic Movement Off-The-Ball (Overlaps, Give & Go’s, "Attacking" Defenders)
  • An overall attractiveness to our play. How we play is as important as the results of our play.

Develop and Grow Players:

  • Trying to win in the immediate future is easier than long-term player development

Educate, Develop, and Attract Fans to our sport

Purpose

Be a Godly example to all we come in contact with, both on and off the field.

Everything you do, big or small, matters!

  • Every little thing is done in the way it must be done

Salt & Light and A City On A Hill.

  • Exhibit a consistent and contagious Christian Testimony in our words, actions, sportsmanship.
  • And by our effort, perseverance, and intentionality in sharing and demonstrating the Gospel.

Pursuit of Excellence is the program’s motto

  • We will seek to pursue this in all areas, on and off the field.
  • Not out of pride or boastfulness, but rather in stewardship; as a call or response to the gifts and opportunities provided for us by the Lord.

Purpose

Provide players with the most memorable, meaningful, and impactful competitive athletic experience of their lives.

Nurture powerful and lasting relationships

Provide impactful and life-changing lessons and spiritual applications

Create an environment where each person in the program matters and everyone senses they are part of something bigger than themselves.

Facilitate the ultimate capstone project!

Strive for 50-year impact, not simply next season.

Purpose

We are looking for the complete player.

We seek to find valuable players who are a great fit for our program, regradless of their percieved value according to other programs.
At TIU the complete player is someone who fits our six criteria:

  1. Selfless
  2. Hard Working
  3. Perseverant
  4. Teachable
  5. Highly Fit
  6. Talented

Who

Selfless

5 simple words: ”It is not about you”.

You are on a team.

When you become part of a team, you give up individual rights and gain team responsibilities.

You are required to die to self. “Unless a seed falls to the ground and dies, it cannot bear fruit.”

The decision to play on this team is a decision to become part of something bigger than yourself.

Selflessness builds. Selfishness destroys.

Our ultimate model in this is Jesus Christ - Philippians 2:5-7

Soccer here at Trinity is, and will always be, played as a team.

Who

Hard Working

Absolutely nothing comes to any of us ‘free of charge’.

I cannot begin to get the most out of you individually and this team collectively, or lead us where I feel the Lord calling us to go without a consistent and relentless work ethic.

It's a stewardship issue:

  • A full use of the talents and opportunities provided to you by the Lord.
  • Proverbs 14:23

Who

Perseverant

Soccer, like life, is filled with obstacles, difficulties, and setbacks.

Knowing that most days, much less weeks, months, and seasons are filled with challenges, we need players who can push through the immediate moment and who, with hopeful resiliency, keep battling toward the promise of a better future.

James 1:2-4

Who

Teachable

None of us are finished products or too good to grow and develop further.

The Two Curses Of Life: Arrogance and Ignorance. Teachability can cure both!

We must have one unified playing style, and you cannot play for us if you’re not willing to listen to the coaching staff and change your game and habits for the team's benefit.

We must have one collective, unified team philosophy, and you cannot play for us if you’re not willing to be challenged by the coaching staff and buy in to the team goals and culture.

Proverbs 22:6

Who

Highly Fit

We are not looking for ‘solid’ or ‘just good enough’ at TIU

We are looking for ‘off-the-charts’, ‘barrier-breaking’, ‘record-setting’, ‘I can’t believe I just did that’ fitness!

I feel that it is fair of me to ask for this and call you to aspire to that level because there really is no clear idea of just how far you can push yourself or go in this area. And more importantly, it is completely under your own control.

So much of athletics and competition is outside of our control. However, fitness and conditioning, so vital in soccer, are actually completely under our control. Control The Controllables!

Who

Talented

We demand that players have a great first touch, skill on the ball, experience, knowledge of tactics and the game, etc.

I would never want to downplay this on my list of desired traits in a complete soccer player. To play this sport collegiately, being strong in the soccer-specific areas is vital and something we want to exist in our program.

We recognize and value special, unique or God-given talent:

  • Vision
  • Decision-Making
  • Speed
  • Size
  • Left-Footed

We are also committed to working hard to ensure that talent is continually being developed in all of our players.

Proverbs 22:29
